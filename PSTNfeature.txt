## 1. Number Ranges (Prefixes)

* Master Ranges: Large blocks of numbers allocated by a telecom provider (e.g., +1-555-0100 to +1-555-0199).
* Sub-ranges: Smaller blocks broken down for specific organizational needs (e.g., a specific department or office floor).
* Prefix Details: The specific country code, area code, or internal routing prefix.

## 2. Individual Telephone Numbers

* DID Numbers: Direct Inward Dialing numbers mapped directly from the public network to internal extensions.
* Internal Extensions: Short-digit dialing extensions used inside an office (e.g., 4-digit or 5-digit local extensions).
* Toll-Free Numbers: Inbound customer service numbers (e.g., 1-800 numbers).

## 3. Allocation and Ownership Details

* Status: The availability state of the number (e.g., Offline, Active, Reserved, Future Use).
* Assigned Users / Owners: The specific employee, team, or department utilizing the number.
* Customer / Client Links: If you are a service provider, you can assign numbers or entire ranges to specific external customers.

## 4. Infrastructure Mapping

* Locations: The physical office building, campus, or data center where the numbers are being routed.
* Devices: The specific hardware or virtual telecom infrastructure (such as a VoIP Gateway, PBX, or Session Border Controller) handling the line.

## 5. Documentation and Auditing

* Descriptions: Custom text fields to note what the number is used for (e.g., "Main Fax Line," "CEO Mobile Forwarding").
* Changelogs / Audit Trails: A historical log detailing who created, edited, or deleted a number or range, along with timestamps.
